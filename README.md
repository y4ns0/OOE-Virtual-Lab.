## I. PHYSICS OF REFRACTION AND APPLICATIONS

* **Q1. The Swimming Pool Phenomenon:** Explain why an object submerged at a depth of 4m appears to be closer to the surface (apparent depth). Use the simplified Snell-Descartes law.
* **Q2. Guidance Error:** If a robotic arm uses a camera without correcting for water refraction (n = 1.33), what is the concrete risk when attempting to grasp an object at the bottom?
* **Q3. Fiber Optics:** What is the role of the critical angle in data transmission via fiber optics? What happens if the angle of incidence is too small?

## II. EYE MODELING AND CORRECTION

* **Q4. The Relaxed Eye:** Why is the optical power of a relaxed eye defined at approximately 66.67 Diopters for an image formed on the retina (15mm)?
* **Q5. Accommodation Effort:** To read a document at 25cm, the crystalline lens must bulge. What impact does this have on its focal length?
* **Q6. The Case of Myopia:** A myopic eye forms the image in front of the retina. Why is a diverging lens the physical solution to this problem?

## III. PROJECTION AND SURVEILLANCE SYSTEMS

* **Q7. Video Projector:** To project an upright image onto a wall, in which direction should the transparency or microfilm be placed in the device? Justify your answer.
* **Q8. The Peephole (Judas):** How does a lens with a power of -10D allow a person to see an entire hallway through a small hole in a door?
* **Q9. Total Reflection:** In the "Laser Grid" project, why do two perpendicular mirrors always reflect the laser beam parallel to its input direction?
